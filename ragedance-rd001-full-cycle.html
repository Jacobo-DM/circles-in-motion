How to Use This Card

RD-001 is a simple, repeatable RageDance cycle you can use alone or with a small group. You can follow it exactly, shorten it, or adapt it to your body and context. The times are suggestions, not rules.

You can practice this on an 18-inch mat, in a bedroom, in a quiet corner, or in a small classroom space. Participation is always voluntary.

RD-001 · Full Cycle

1. Arrival & Breath (1–2 minutes)

  • Stand or sit. Notice your feet or the place your body meets the ground.
  • Take 5–10 slow breaths: in through the nose, out through the mouth.
  • Let your exhale be slightly longer than your inhale.

2. Body Check-In (1–2 minutes)

  • Scan from head to toes: Where is there tightness, heat, numbness, or buzzing?
  • You can quietly name it: “Right now I feel… tight in my chest / heavy in my legs / hot in my jaw.”
  • No need to fix anything. Just notice.

3. Neck & Facial Tapping (1–2 minutes)

  • Use fingertips to tap gently along your jaw, cheeks, temples, and the back of your neck.
  • Let your jaw drop a little; unclench your teeth.
  • Exhale with sound if it helps: a sigh, a hum, a low growl.

4. Shoulder Circles (1–2 minutes)

  • Roll shoulders forward 10–20 times, then backward 10–20 times.
  • Let arms hang heavy; imagine weight draining out through your fingertips.
  • Stay within a pain-free range. Smaller is fine.

5. Qigong Sway + Hip Rhythm (2–3 minutes)

  • Let your weight shift slowly from side to side, like a relaxed sway.
  • Allow your hips to follow — small circles or figure eights.
  • If you have music, match the sway to the rhythm. If not, sway to your own breath.

6. Twin Infinity Loops (2–3 minutes)

  • Imagine drawing a sideways “8” (∞) in front of you with your hands or elbows.
  • Let the movement cross the midline of your body each time.
  • Work one side for a while, then switch. Smooth, continuous, like tracing currents in water.

7. Elbow Arc Drills (2–3 minutes)

  • From a grounded stance, let one elbow arc up and across your body, then gently return.
  • Alternate sides. Think of each arc as drawing a protective curve in the air.
  • Keep movements contained and precise — power is less important than clarity.

8. Pulse Reflection & Exit (1–2 minutes)

  • Return to stillness or a soft sway.
  • Notice your breath, heart rate, temperature, and the weight in your limbs.
  • Quietly name one thing that feels even a little different now.
  • End with one steady breath in, one long breath out.
Remember: There is no “perfect” version of RD-001. If you only have 5 minutes, choose 3–4 steps. If you feel overwhelmed, slow down, sit, or stop. Your agency comes first.
