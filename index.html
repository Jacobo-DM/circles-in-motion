RageDance

Breath. Softening.
A place where strong feeling can move.

Before anything else — a breath
  1. Stand or sit. Feel the ground beneath you.
  2. Inhale slowly through the nose. Let the ribs expand.
  3. Exhale through the mouth. Let the jaw unclench.
  4. Roll your shoulders forward once, then back. Let them drop.
  5. Notice: something has already softened.

That is RageDance. You just began.

RageDance is a way to move anger, grief, pressure, and adrenaline through the body — without harming yourself or others. It is a small-space practice. You can do it on an 18-inch mat, in a bedroom, a corner of a classroom, or under an open sky.

The goal is not to get rid of what you feel. The goal is to give it rhythm, breath, and a clear path through the body so you can carry it with more steadiness and less harm.

No performance. No competition. No correct body. You arrive as you are. The only real requirement is honesty.
