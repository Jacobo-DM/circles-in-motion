# Circles in Motion

Circles in Motion is a framework for movement practice and embodied inquiry.

It holds distinct branches that share a common principle: circular motion as a regulator. Energy is directed, returned, and integrated — never forced, never trapped. The work happens in the body before it is named.

This is an umbrella, not a method. Branches within Circles in Motion develop in their own voices, with their own histories and their own paths, while resting on shared ground in regulation, presence, and return.

---

## What lives here

```text
Circles in Motion  (umbrella framework)
│
├── Branches
│   ├── RageDance              — the originating fire thread
│   └── Baile de los Círculos  — a water / return thread
│
├── Practices
│   └── Firewheel              — a bilateral spiral practice; used across branches
│
└── Shared materials
    ├── declaration, ethics, lineage
    ├── warmups, sequences, patterns
    └── field notes
````

**RageDance** transforms emotional intensity — grief, rage, pressure — into circular flow without domination or collapse. Its emphasis is not aggression; it is return.

**Baile de los Círculos** is grounded in the principle that every gesture expands from and returns to center. It prioritizes redirection over collision, resilience over rigidity. It is developed in Spanish and may be adapted in other languages while preserving its intent.

Each branch has its own history, language, and development path. They are not variants of one method. They share principles without being interchangeable.

**Firewheel** is a bilateral spiral practice drawn from live sessions. It is held here as exploratory, usable across branches rather than belonging to one.

Additional warm-ups, sequences, patterns, and field notes are shared as they become ready. Materials evolve through use.

## What this is

* A framework for movement grounded in regulation, presence, and return.
* A home for distinct branches that share common principles.
* A working space. Practices are shared early and refined in the body, not on paper.
* Held in right relation to the Flower Road Commons — see `declaration.html` and `ethics.html`.

## What this is not

* Not a curriculum. Not a certification program. Not a brand.
* Not a performance system. The work is not designed to make anyone look powerful.
* Not a synonym for RageDance. RageDance is one branch within this framework, not the whole.
* Not a method to master. This work does not require prior expertise to begin.

## A note on use

Circles in Motion rejects domination, spectacle, and coercion. It is not designed to impress.

It is designed to help people stay regulated and present — to themselves, to each other, and to what they are moving through.

Adaptations are welcome. Preserve the originating intent of the branch you are working with: circular motion as a regulator, not a performance. Preserve lineage where you find it.

For anyone teaching or practicing, see the ethics and practice agreements.
