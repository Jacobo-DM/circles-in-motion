**Circles in Motion** is an evolving movement practice grounded in the principle of circular return.

Every motion extends outward and returns to center.  
Strikes do not terminate in tension; they flow back into the body.  
Steps spiral rather than collide.  
Breath and posture remain integrated throughout.

Energy flows like water — never trapped, never forced, always in motion, always able to move again.

## Framework

The practice develops:

- Somatic awareness  
- Emotional regulation through movement  
- Balance, coordination, and spatial intelligence  
- Non-aggressive strength generation  
- Adaptability across standing and seated forms  

Movements are continuous and cyclical, emphasizing redirection over impact and resilience over rigidity.

## Application

Circles in Motion can be practiced:

- Individually  
- In classrooms  
- In community settings  
- Within wellness, expressive arts, or resilience-based programs  

The framework is modular and adaptable across cultures while preserving its core circular principles.

## Lineage & Branches

- **RageDance** — the originating lineage focused on transmuting emotional intensity into circular flow. (See: `branches/ragedance.md`)
- **Baile de los Círculos** — Spanish-language and culturally adaptive branch. (See: `branches/baile-de-los-circulos.md`)
- Additional branches may emerge through ethical dialogue and responsible adaptation.

## Guiding Principle

Circular motion builds resilience without reinforcing domination patterns.

## Ethical Use

Circles in Motion is open for study and adaptation in educational, community, and artistic contexts.

Preserve lineage.  
Maintain non-dominant intent.  
Do not weaponize.

## Technology & Adaptation

Circles in Motion is an embodied practice.

Digital tools — including AI-assisted documentation, reflection, translation, or accessibility supports — may be used in service of the practice. Technology does not replace lived movement; it supports learning, access, and continuity.

Embodiment remains primary.
